Open Robust Compartmentalization Alliance (ORCA)

A neutral, open foundation for people building real-world isolation and compartmentalization into software systems.

What is ORCA?

ORCA is a new foundation focused on advancing practical software isolation and compartmentalization. For too long, a single fault or failure in software has been enough to trigger large-scale disruptions and security incidents.

Our goal is to help the community build and deploy defense-in-depth techniques so that bugs are harder to turn into working exploits and their impact is limited when they do occur.

Why now?

Cyber attacks are increasing in scale and speed, and AI is making it cheaper to probe and exploit systems. Today, we invest heavily in reducing the likelihood of vulnerabilities being exploitable.

ORCA focuses on the other half of the equation: reducing the impact. Robust compartmentalization ensures that a single compromise does not lead to total system failure.

How to get involved

  • Everyone is welcome. We are a broad community spanning hardware, operating systems, programming languages, user-space runtimes, and applied security.
  • Bring your use cases. We are especially interested in early adopters who want to try out isolation and compartmentalization techniques in real deployments.
  • Stay in the loop. We are organizing a community kick-off meeting and will share details as they are finalized.
